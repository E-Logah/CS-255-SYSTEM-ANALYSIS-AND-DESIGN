# CS-255-SYSTEM-ANALYSIS-AND-DESIGN
## Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
DriverPass was the client for this project. The company wanted a system that would help students better prepare for their driving tests by providing online access to practice exams, scheduling driving lessons, tracking student progress, and managing user accounts. The system also needed to integrate with DMV information so that students could access current testing materials. The goal was to create a secure, user-friendly web-based system that supported students, instructors, administrators, and the business owner.
## What did you do particularly well?
One of the strongest aspects of my work was gathering and translating customer requirements into system designs. I created business requirements, use case diagrams, activity diagrams, sequence diagrams, and class diagrams that represented the needs of the client and the different user roles within the system. I also focused on identifying both functional and nonfunctional requirements, including security, performance, and usability considerations.
## If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I could revise one area of my work, I would improve the UML diagrams by adding more detailed relationships and system interactions. While the diagrams accurately represented the system requirements, I believe additional detail would make the design easier for developers and stakeholders to understand. I would also improve some diagram descriptions to provide more explanation about how each component contributes to the overall system.
## How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I interpreted the user's needs by carefully reviewing the DriverPass interview transcript and identifying the features the client considered most important. These requirements were then incorporated into the business requirements document and system design models. Considering the user's needs is critical because software is ultimately created to solve business problems and support users. If the design does not align with user expectations, the final product may not meet business goals or provide value to customers.
## How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
My approach to software design begins with understanding stakeholder requirements and defining the business problem. I then identify functional and nonfunctional requirements and create models that describe system behavior and structure. Techniques that I would continue using include requirements analysis, use case modeling, UML diagrams, activity diagrams, sequence diagrams, and class diagrams. In future projects, I would place additional emphasis on iterative feedback and Agile practices to ensure the system continues to align with stakeholder expectations throughout development.
